Creating Graphics With Figma

MAY 31, 2023 • BY PROBABLYRAGING

Figma is a browser-based interface primarily used for product design and as a mock-up tool, but it can also produce some awesome graphics that you can use in your videos and other places. For this demonstration, I'm going to create a simple subscriber CTA (call to action) that you can import directly into your video editing suite to use in your videos as a basic on-screen image, or into a program like Adobe After Effects to give it some life by adding animation to it. Figma also comes as a desktop app if you're not a fan of doing everything in your browser.

 

Creating A New Design

Firstly, head over to www.figma.com and register a free account. Once you're all signed up and logged in you should be on the dashboard page where you can create a new file or view your previous projects. Let's start by creating a new file by clicking "New design file".

 

 

Create A Base To Work With

CTAs are supposed to catch a viewer's attention and remind them to complete an action, all while being non-intrusive to your content. You can get as fancy with the design as you like, are you can pick a theme that suits your channel style. To keep things simple I'm going to use a white rectangle with rounded corners.

Choose the rectangle shape tool from the tool menu in the top left portion of the window

 

 

Draw a rectangle that is roughly 350x80 on the canvas by click-dragging your mouse

 

 

With your shape drawn on the canvas, choose the layer by clicking on it. Now let's change its color and give it some rounded corners by increasing the corner radius value in the options panel on the left side of the window. I went with a corner radius of 30

 

 

Add A Subscribe Button

Now that we have our base rectangle, it's time to add a subscribe button. We're going to use the rectangle tool again, this time making the size 100x25, with a red fill and a corner radius of 8.

Let's add the "subscribe" text to our button by choosing the text tool and clicking anywhere within our button rectangle

 

 

Add Your Brand

Let's make this unique by adding some branding. Things like your channel icon, channel name, and current subscriber count. Choose the ellipse shape tool by clicking the down arrow next to the rectangle shape tool we used earlier.

 

 

Now we can draw a circle that is 64x64 within our base rectangle. We don't need to worry about the fill color as we're going to cover it with our channel icon image next

 

 

To add our channel icon image, we first need to import the file into our Figma project by clicking on the menu option > file >place image, and selecting our image file

 

 

Once you pick your image file, the image will be attached to your mouse cursor, ready to be placed. We want to place to image inside of the ellipse shape we just created, it's as simple as clicking the ellipse shape on your canvas, and the image will automatically be placed within that shape layer

 

 

Adding out channel name and subscriber count is going to be pretty straightforward. We just use the text tool we used earlier, place out text where we like, and give it some style. Here is what I did with mine

 

 

Exporting Our Project

Now that we have our CTA finished and looking good, we want to export our file. There are a few file types to choose from when exporting from Figma but we're going to go with PNG for our file type.

If you're just wanting a simple image to use in your videos, you can export the entire project as a single image file, but if you're wanting to add some animation to your project, you will likely want to export each layer individually. Regardless of what you choose, the export process is going to be the same.

To export your project you will need to select all your layers. You can do this by click-dragging on the canvas, or simply by using the CTRL+A keyboard shortcut

 

 

Once you have all your layers selected, you want to right-click anywhere within your CTA > copy/paste as > copy as PNG

 

 

Now that we have all your layers copied as a PNG, we want to paste the new PNG layer onto our canvas. We first want to deselect all the layers we previously selected. Once we've done that, we can right-click anywhere on the canvas and select "paste here", or you can use the CTRL+V keyboard shortcut

 

 

You should now have a PNG layer labelled "image 1" that looks identical to all of our layers

 

 

To export our new PNG layer, we can click on the layer, and in the options panel on the right side of the screen, we can go to "Export" and make sure the PNG file type is selected, and then click the "export image 1" button

 

 

The Result

There you have it. You just used Figma to create a cool CTA that you can use in your videos to remind people to subscribe to your content. You can take it one step further by downloading each individual layer as a PNG file and importing them into a program such as After Effects to add some animations like mouse clicks and swipe in/out transitions.
